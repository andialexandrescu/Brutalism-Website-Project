Le Corbusier

Fact Description
Full Name Charles-Edouard Jeanneret-Gris
Birthdate October 6, 1887
Birthplace La Chaux-de-Fonds, Switzerland
Death August 27, 1965 in Roquebrune-Cap-Martin, France
Nationality Swiss, French
Significant Contributions Pioneer of modern architecture, designed buildings in Europe, Japan, India, North and South America
Notable Projects Villa Savoye, Villa La Roche, Unite d'habitation, Notre Dame du Haut, Chandigarh master plan
Influence Pioneer of modern architecture, founder of the Congres International d'Architecture Moderne (CIAM), and influential in urban planning
Legacy Corbusier left a lasting legacy that extended beyond architectural boundaries. His career was marked by a diverse range of creations, including over 300 designs that aimed to improve living conditions and society through housing. Le Corbusier's work encompassed not only architecture but also drawings, paintings, sculptures, furniture design, and theoretical writings.

Quote

Architecture is the masterly, correct and magnificent play of masses brought together in light. Our eyes are made to see forms in light; light and shade reveal these forms; cubes, cones, spheres, cylinders or pyramids are the great primary forms which light reveals to advantage; the image of these is distinct and tangible within us without ambiguity. It is for this reason that these are beautiful forms, the most beautiful forms. Everybody is agreed to that, the child, the savage and the metaphysician.

Towards an Architecture (1923)

Marcel Breuer

Fact Description
Full Name Marcel Lajos Breuer
Birthdate May 21, 1902
Birthplace Pecs, Austria-Hungary
Death July 1, 1981 in New York, United States
Nationality Hungarian, German, American
Significant Contributions Designed the Wassily Chair and the Cesca Chair at the Bauhaus - Notable for his work in art museums, libraries, college buildings, and residences - Designed the former IBM Research and Development facility, birthplace of the first personal computer
Notable Projects Grote Residence, Dessau, Germany; Moholy-Nagy Apartment and Studio, Berlin, Germany; Muche House, Dessau, Germany
Influence AIA Gold Medal (1968); Known for tubular steel furniture pieces - Emphasized simplicity, functionality, and innovative use of materials in architecture and design - Iconic symbol of modern design history
Legacy Criticism of urban planning ideas, alleged ties with fascism, antisemitism, eugenics

Quote

I am as much interested in the smallest detail as in the whole structure.

Paul Rudolph

Fact Description
Full Name Paul Marvin Rudolph
Birthdate October 23, 1918
Birthplace Elkton, Kentucky, US
Death August 8, 1997 in New York City, US
Nationality American
Significant Contributions Pioneer of modern architecture, designed buildings in Europe, Japan, India, North and South America
Notable Projects W. R. Healy Guest House (The Cocoon House), Tuskegee University Chapel
Influence Rudolph's innovative designs, brutalist concrete and highly complex floor plans, have left a lasting impact on contemporary architecture. His work continues to be celebrated, with his personal archive donated to the Library of Congress and his residence in Manhattan designated as a New York City Landmark
Legacy Yale School of Art's innovative design showcases his expertise in Brutalism, with the exterior featuring corduroy concrete, a distinctive hallmark of the style. But without sacrificing his predilection for architectural rigor, he was also able to create houses that embodied the optimism, and even the glamour, of the postwar era.

Quote

We desperately need to relearn the art of disposing our buildings to create different kinds of space: the quiet, enclosed, isolated, shaded space; the hustling, bustling space, pungent with vitality; the paved, dignified, vast, sumptuous, even awe-inspiring space; the mysterious space; the transition space which defines, separates, and yet joins juxtaposed spaces of contrasting character. We need sequences of space which arouse one’s curiosity, give a sense of anticipation, which beckon and impel us to rush forward to find that releasing space which dominates, which acts as a climax and magnet, and gives direction.

Architectural Education In The United States - lecture (1961)

Erno Goldfinger

Fact Description
Full Name Erno Goldfinger
Birthdate September 11, 1902
Birthplace Budapest, Hungary
Death November 15, 1987 in London, England
Nationality Hungarian, British
Significant Contributions Pioneer of modern architecture, designed buildings in Europe, including the UK, and influenced urban planning in London through his residential tower blocks
Notable Projects 2 Willow Road, Alexander Fleming House, Balfron Tower, Carradale House, Trellick Tower
Influence Known for being a key member of the Modernist architectural movement, designing residential tower blocks and brutalist structures, he was strongly influenced by Le Corbusier and Auguste Perret.
Legacy Erno Goldfinger's legacy is deeply rooted in his pioneering contributions to modern architecture, particularly through his innovative designs of residential tower blocks that have become iconic landmarks in London's skyline.

Quote

The spatial order is built up by an amalgamation of a multitude of phenomena, the perception of which, subconsciously integrated, helps in building up the sensation of space. Memories and experience, not only of visual sensation but also of sound and touch and smell, enter into it. The sound and vibration in a hall; the physical touch of the walls of a narrow passage; the atmosphere and temperature of a stuffy room; the smell of a damp cellar; all are, in various degrees, components of spatial sensation. Every element, plastic or pictorial, partially obstructing the view, and people in the crowd rubbing against you, are part of it.

The Sensation of Space (1941)
